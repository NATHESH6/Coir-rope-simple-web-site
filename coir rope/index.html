Coconut Fiber

What is Coir?

Coir, also known as coconut fiber, is a natural fiber extracted from the husk of coconuts. It's the fibrous material found between the hard, internal shell and the outer coat of a coconut.

For centuries, coir has been used to make products such as floor mats, doormats, brushes, mattresses, and ropes due to its durability and water-resistant properties.

Coir Processing Methods

Traditional craftsmanship meets modern sustainability

01

Retting

Coconut husks are soaked in water for 6-12 months to soften the fibers through natural fermentation.

6-12 months Natural water retting
02

Fiber Extraction

After retting, fibers are separated from the husk using traditional beating or mechanical decorticators.

Manual beating Mechanical extraction
03

Spinning

Extracted fibers are spun into yarn using traditional spinning wheels or modern machines.

Traditional spinning Machine spinning
04

Rope Making

Spun yarn is twisted and braided into ropes of various thicknesses using hand or machine techniques.

Hand braiding Machine braiding
